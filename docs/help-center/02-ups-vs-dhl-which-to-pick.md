# UPS vs DHL: which one to pick for which destination

> Audience: forwarding partners · Estimated read time: 3 minutes

Both UPS and DHL cover 190+ countries on BridgeLogis, but they're not
interchangeable. Picking the right one in the first ten seconds of a quote
saves margin and avoids surprises at pickup. Here's the short version.

## The short answer

- **UPS** wins on most North American lanes, industrial destinations, and
  shipments that need rigid dimensional checks at pickup.
- **DHL** wins on most European lanes, remote-area fringes, and
  time-critical deliveries under 5 kg.
- **Neither wins everywhere** — run the quote and compare side-by-side.
  BridgeLogis shows both tariffs with the same billable weight, FX, and
  FSC so the comparison is fair.

## When to lean UPS

- **United States, Canada** — UPS zones are tighter and the ground
  network in both countries is deeper.
- **Latin America non-capital cities** — UPS's clearance network in
  Mexico, Brazil, and Argentina is more predictable.
- **Heavy or dimensional cargo (>30 kg actual)** — UPS's range table
  flattens faster than DHL's for bulk express.
- **Middle East via the Surge Fee lanes** — UPS applies a published
  surge fee per kg that BridgeLogis auto-detects; DHL often prices these
  lanes case-by-case.

## When to lean DHL

- **Western Europe** — DHL's Bonn hub and direct feeders from Incheon
  mean cheaper transit on Z6 countries (Germany, France, UK, Italy,
  Spain) for most weights.
- **Remote areas** — DHL's remote-area fee schedule is consistent across
  the globe; UPS's EAS/RAS logic varies.
- **Small shipments under 5 kg** — DHL's rate steps for under-10 kg are
  generally softer.
- **Israel and select Middle East** — DHL avoids the UPS surge fee
  entirely on some lanes.

## When it's a coin flip

- **Intra-Asia (Japan, Taiwan, Singapore, Vietnam, Thailand)** — often
  within 5–10% of each other. Let BridgeLogis tell you which is cheaper
  for your exact weight.
- **Australia, New Zealand** — close race. UPS sometimes edges ahead on
  the range table, DHL on the exact table.
- **Africa** — price depends heavily on whether the destination is a
  capital city. Always quote both.

## Things to watch for on any lane

- **EAS/RAS/DAS postal code surcharges** — BridgeLogis automatically
  detects these for UPS based on zip code. Always double-check the
  banner in the UPS add-on panel before sending the quote.
- **DHL OSP/OWT auto-detection** — for oversized or overweight pieces,
  DHL adds surcharges automatically. You'll see them itemized in the
  quote breakdown.
- **Lithium batteries (ELI/ELM)** — these require a separate dangerous
  goods quote and are not priced on BridgeLogis. Ask our team.
